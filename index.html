SANKƆFA(SANKOFA), the timeless Ghanaian principle that urges us to “go back and fetch what we may have forgotten”, is the heartbeat of Ndisco Community. It is the reminder that our future shines brightest when we carry the wisdom, culture, and identity of our past with us.

Through a powerful blend of storytelling, podcasts, YouTube content, mentorship, and community-led resources, Ndisco serves as a gateway for young Africans to rediscover who they are, celebrate where they come from, and chart new paths for growth.

The community curates conversations on culture, tradition, history, creativity, and real-life experiences—bringing together voices from across generations. Ndisco empowers individuals to learn, reflect, and engage deeply with issues that shape the African journey at home and abroad.

Meet The Convener

Dr

Marcellina

Oparaoji

is a respected educationist, global industry leader, and the convener of the Ndisco Community. Originally from Imo State, Nigeria and now based in the United States. she has built an exceptional career within one of the world’s largest pharmaceutical organizations, where her leadership continues to influence health outcomes across markets.

Driven by a deep passion for culture, heritage, and excellence, Dr. Oparaoji founded Ndisco Community to support African youths, young adults, and diaspora families navigating cultural identity, mentorship, and personal development. Her work is inspired by her own experience as an immigrant mother, a journey she explores in her book, “Raising an African Child in America: From the Perspective of an Immigrant Nigerian Mom.”

She is married to Professor Eddie Oparaoji, a fellow pharmacist and academic leader. Together, they exemplify dedication to family, community, and generational upliftment. Through Ndisco Community, Dr. Oparaoji continues to champion cultural pride, guidance, and excellence for Africans at home and in the diaspora.
